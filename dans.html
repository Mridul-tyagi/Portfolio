Project Overview

The Dubai Air Navigation System (DANS) is a critical government entity responsible for air traffic control and navigation services across the UAE. The existing website was outdated, difficult to navigate, and not optimized for mobile users. The goal was to create a modern, accessible, and user-friendly corporate website that serves both aviation professionals and the general public.

As part of a cross-functional design team, I took full ownership of designing 40+ tablet and mobile screens, ensuring a seamless experience across all devices while adhering to strict client branding and UI/UX guidelines. The project required deep understanding of aviation terminology and user needs.

Pain Points
"Finding critical aviation documents on the current website takes too long. We need a more organized structure with better search functionality."
— Captain Mohammed Al Ansari, Senior Air Traffic Controller
"The website doesn't work well on my phone. As someone who travels frequently, I need access to flight statistics and announcements on the go."

Key Research Findings: The existing website had a 68% bounce rate on mobile devices. Users reported spending an average of 5-7 minutes finding specific documents. 75% of aviation professionals accessed the site from mobile devices but found the experience frustrating. The content hierarchy was unclear, and the bilingual support (Arabic/English) was inconsistent.

Key Design Decisions

📱 1. Mobile-First Responsive Design

75% of users accessed the site from mobile devices. We prioritized mobile design first, then expanded to tablet and desktop layouts.

❌ Before

Desktop-only design, unreadable on mobile, pinch-to-zoom required

✅ After

Fully responsive with touch-friendly navigation, fluid grids, and optimized typography

🌐 2. Seamless Bilingual Experience (RTL/LTR)

Arabic users needed equal access. We designed a true RTL layout with proper typography and cultural considerations.

❌ Before

Machine-translated Arabic, broken layouts, inconsistent navigation

✅ After

Native RTL support, mirrored layouts, proper Arabic typography, seamless language toggle

🔍 3. Advanced Search & Filtering

Users struggled to find documents. We implemented a powerful search with filters for document type, date, and category.

❌ Before

Basic search with irrelevant results, no filters, 5-7 minutes average search time

✅ After

Advanced search with auto-suggest, filters, and 30-second average document access

Iterations made after testing: Simplified the document center navigation based on user feedback. Added a sticky search bar for quick access. Design Process

🔍 Phase 1: Research & Discovery

Conducted stakeholder interviews with DANS officials to understand content priorities. Analyzed competitor aviation websites and gathered user feedback from aviation professionals. Created detailed user personas for both public users and industry professionals.

📱 Phase 2: Mobile-First Wireframing

Started with mobile wireframes to ensure critical content works on small screens. Created low-fidelity prototypes for 40+ mobile screens, then expanded to tablet and desktop layouts. Focused on thumb-friendly navigation and touch targets.

🎨 Phase 3: High-Fidelity Design

Translated wireframes into pixel-perfect high-fidelity mockups using Figma. Applied DANS branding guidelines, created a component library for consistency, and designed responsive layouts for all 60+ screens. Paid special attention to data tables and complex information hierarchy.

✅ Phase 4: Testing & Handoff

Conducted usability testing with 12 users across different device types. Iterated based on feedback and prepared detailed design specs for developers. Worked alongside the development team during implementation to ensure 100% pixel-perfect execution.
